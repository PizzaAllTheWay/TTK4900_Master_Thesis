\subsection{Introduction}
In contrast to complex factors such as odometry or landmark constraints that require state propagation, data association, or nonlinear optimization, some sensor measurements can be directly converted into factor graph constraints with minimal processing.  
\\ \\
These measurements are referred to as \textit{``direct factors''}. They give direct information about the vehicle state and are usually added as unary factors connected to a single pose node. For AUVs, the most important direct factor is the depth factor, since depth measurements are normally available during most of the mission and provide a strong constraint on the vertical position of the vehicle. GNSS can also be used as an additional direct factor when the AUV is at the surface, where it provides absolute position and heading measurements from the dual antenna GNSS system.
\\ \\
Depth and GNSS measurements are used as supporting unary factors that can be attached directly to the relevant pose nodes when available. This allows simple absolute measurements to help constrain the estimate without adding extra complexity to the graph structure.
